Hypertension is commonly treated with medications such as Lisinopril, Amlodipine, Hydrochlorothiazide, and Losartan, which help lower blood pressure and reduce the risk of heart disease. Type 2 Diabetes is managed with drugs like Metformin, Glipizide, Sitagliptin, and insulin to control blood sugar levels. Asthma treatment includes inhaled corticosteroids like Fluticasone, bronchodilators such as Salbutamol, and leukotriene receptor antagonists like Montelukast to reduce airway inflammation and improve breathing. Hyperlipidemia is typically treated with statins such as Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin, and Simvastatin to lower LDL cholesterol and prevent cardiovascular disease. Depression may be managed with antidepressants like Fluoxetine, Sertraline, Citalopram, and Escitalopram, which help regulate neurotransmitters in the brain. Bacterial infections are treated with antibiotics including Amoxicillin, Ciprofloxacin, Azithromycin, and Doxycycline, depending on the type and site of infection. Allergic rhinitis is managed with antihistamines like Loratadine, Cetirizine, and Fexofenadine, along with corticosteroids such as Fluticasone nasal spray when necessary. Osteoarthritis is addressed with NSAIDs such as Ibuprofen, Naproxen, and Diclofenac, and occasionally with intra-articular steroid injections. GERD is treated using proton pump inhibitors like Omeprazole, Pantoprazole, and Esomeprazole to reduce stomach acid. Tuberculosis requires a combination therapy of Isoniazid, Rifampin, Ethambutol, and Pyrazinamide over 6 to 9 months. Epilepsy is managed with antiepileptic drugs such as Valproic Acid, Carbamazepine, Levetiracetam, and Lamotrigine. Migraines are treated with Triptans (e.g., Sumatriptan), NSAIDs, and prophylactic drugs like Propranolol and Topiramate. COVID-19 is managed with antivirals like Remdesivir and supportive therapies including corticosteroids (e.g., Dexamethasone) and supplemental oxygen. HIV/AIDS is treated with antiretroviral therapy, involving medications like Tenofovir, Emtricitabine, Efavirenz, and Dolutegravir. Anxiety disorders may be addressed with medications such as Diazepam, Alprazolam, Buspirone, or SSRIs including Sertraline and Paroxetine. Iron-deficiency anemia is treated with Ferrous Sulfate or IV iron, while B12 deficiency anemia requires cyanocobalamin injections. Psoriasis treatment includes topical corticosteroids, vitamin D analogs like Calcipotriene, systemic agents such as Methotrexate, and biologics like Adalimumab. Schizophrenia is managed using antipsychotic medications like Olanzapine, Risperidone, Clozapine, and Aripiprazole. Chronic kidney disease management may involve Erythropoietin-stimulating agents, phosphate binders, diuretics, and ACE inhibitors to support renal function and overall health.
